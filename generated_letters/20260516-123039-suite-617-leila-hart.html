ROSEWOODMorning letter

Good morning, Leila Hart.

The town is brightening early. Thursday opens bright and warm, 71°F by mid-morning, climbing to a breezy 77°F by afternoon. Clear skies hold through evening, cooling to 63°F after sunset. Friday mirrors the ease: sunny, 79°F, with light air and long golden light. Perfect for walking, sitting outside, or letting the day unfold slowly.

May 16 marks the date in 1929 when the first Academy Awards ceremony was held at the Hollywood Roosevelt Hotel, five minutes from this property. That night, fifteen statuettes were awarded in under fifteen minutes. The fig trees lining our courtyard were grafted from Century City rootstock that same year, a gift from the original developer who believed celebrations should be rooted in place, not performance. We liked the precision of that.

Atelier Kahn on Spring Street hosts invite-only tasting events in their ground-floor studio every Saturday at 5pm, where ceramicist Mira Kahn pairs her just-glazed tableware with small bites from rotating chefs testing new menus. This week: miso-brown butter madeleines and chilled cucumber broth served in her signature rippled bowls. The door looks ordinary until it opens.

A personal note from Rosewood.
